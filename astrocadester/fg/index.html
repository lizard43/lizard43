why is this hard?

The Astrocade arcade platform was not a consumer development target. Bally/Midway and Dave Nutting Associates (DNA) built commercial embedded games, shipped ROMs, and documented the machines mainly so operators could keep them running.

The result is an archaeological dig. Resources like BallyAlley have *tons* of great info but most is targeted to the Astrocade console and not the Astrocade Arcade Harware platform.

Service manuals tells us what the board is. MAME tells you how the emulator sees it. The console manuals tell you what the custom chips were meant to do. Robby Roto and Wizard of Wor disassemblies tell you how commercial games actually used them. Real hardware tells you which assumptions survived contact with the cabinet.

Not console Astrocade

The home system knowledge matters, especially Magic, palette, and sound concepts. But arcade machines add card racks, different input routing, pattern boards, protected RAM behavior, speech hardware, and hi-res video assumptions.

Not MAME either

MAME is the best executable specification we have; this guide distinguishes emulator-confirmed behavior from schematic and from real-hardware.
